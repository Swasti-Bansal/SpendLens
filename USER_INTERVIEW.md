# User Interviews

## Interview 1 — Anonymous Founder, Small Startup Team

**Date:** 2026-05-26  
**Duration:** ~12 minutes  
**How contacted:** Personal network

### Background
Works in a small startup team focused on development and design workflows. Their team regularly uses AI tools for coding, collaboration, presentations, and productivity tasks.

### Questions & Responses

#### 1. What AI tools does your team currently pay for?
They majorly use Claude and Codex for coding, development, and problem solving tasks. For design work and presentations, they rely on Figma, Canva, and Gamma to improve workflow speed and collaboration.

#### 2. Do you know your exact monthly spend on AI tools off the top of your head?
Not the exact figure, but they have a rough idea of where most of the budget goes. They focus more on productivity value than tracking every subscription cost in detail.

#### 3. Have you ever felt like you might be overpaying but weren't sure how to verify it?
Yes. Over time, teams may continue paying for plans or features they no longer fully use. Without visibility into usage, it becomes difficult to know whether the current plan is still cost effective.

#### 4. How do you currently decide which plan to be on — is it intentional or just whatever you signed up for?
Plans are usually selected based on immediate project requirements and reviewed later depending on actual usage, feature requirements, and productivity impact.

#### 5. If a free tool showed you exactly where you're overspending and how much you could save, would you use it? What would make you trust it?
Yes. They would trust the tool more if recommendations were transparent, supported by real usage insights, and focused on helping make informed decisions rather than simply recommending the cheapest option.

#### 6. What would make you share it with your team or on social media?
If the tool consistently provided accurate insights, reduced unnecessary spending, and simplified decision making, they would comfortably recommend it to others.

#### 7. When it comes to web tools you use, what kind of UI do you prefer?
A clean and intuitive interface where the most important information is visible at a glance. The UI should simplify complex information without feeling cluttered.

### Most Surprising Thing
Even active AI users often do not know their exact monthly spend and rely more on perceived productivity than actual cost tracking.

### What It Changed About My Design
This interview highlighted the importance of simplicity, transparency, and a clean interface that makes financial insights easy to understand quickly.

## Interview 2 — Engineering Team Member, Mid-size Company

**Date:** 2026-05-26  
**Duration:** ~10 minutes  
**How contacted:** LinkedIn

### Background
Part of an engineering-focused team using AI tools for coding, documentation, and productivity tasks across multiple departments.

### Questions & Responses

#### 1. What AI tools does your team currently pay for?
The team mainly uses ChatGPT, GitHub Copilot, and occasionally Notion AI depending on team requirements.

#### 2. Do you know your exact monthly spend on AI tools off the top of your head?
Not exactly. They know the organization pays for several subscriptions but cannot recall the exact monthly amount without checking.

#### 3. Have you ever felt like you might be overpaying but weren't sure how to verify it?
Yes. It often feels like some plans or subscriptions are underutilized, but tracking usage and spending accurately is difficult.

#### 4. How do you currently decide which plan to be on — is it intentional or just whatever you signed up for?
Most upgrades happen reactively when someone hits a usage limit, and those plans continue until someone reviews them later.

#### 5. If a free tool showed you exactly where you're overspending and how much you could save, would you use it? What would make you trust it?
Yes. Simplicity and transparency would increase trust, especially if the tool clearly explained calculations without requiring sensitive access initially.

#### 6. What would make you share it with your team or on social media?
Useful insights, practical savings, and clean presentation of results would encourage sharing.

#### 7. When it comes to web tools you use, what kind of UI do you prefer?
A clean and simple dashboard that is easy to navigate without overwhelming users with excessive data.

### Most Surprising Thing
The organization handled AI subscriptions in a reactive way rather than through structured planning or regular audits.

### What It Changed About My Design
This interview encouraged building actionable recommendations and focusing on usability over overly complex analytics dashboards.

## Interview 3 — Product & Operations Professional, Growing Team

**Date:** 2026-05-27  
**Duration:** ~15 minutes  
**How contacted:** Professional connection

### Background
Works with teams using AI tools across engineering, operations, research, and content workflows. Their organization uses multiple AI subscriptions and API services simultaneously.

### Questions & Responses

#### 1. What AI tools does your team currently pay for?
The team uses OpenAI ChatGPT, GitHub Copilot, Notion AI, Claude by Anthropic, Canva AI tools, and API credits from providers like Google Gemini and Perplexity AI.

#### 2. Do you know your exact monthly spend on AI tools off the top of your head?
Most teams usually do not know the exact monthly spend because subscriptions are fragmented across departments, reimbursement systems, and multiple cards.

#### 3. Have you ever felt like you might be overpaying but weren't sure how to verify it?
Yes. Common issues include overlapping subscriptions, underutilized premium plans, inactive seats, and temporary upgrades that remain active long-term.

#### 4. How do you currently decide which plan to be on — is it intentional or just whatever you signed up for?
Most organizations follow an unstructured approach where users upgrade after hitting limits, and others later adopt the same plan without regular evaluation.

#### 5. If a free tool showed you exactly where you're overspending and how much you could save, would you use it? What would make you trust it?
Yes. Trust would increase if the tool clearly explained calculations, remained vendor-neutral, showed assumptions transparently, and avoided aggressive upselling.

#### 6. What would make you share it with your team or on social media?
Users would likely share the tool if it generated useful reports, visually appealing insights, or a strong realization about unnecessary spending.

#### 7. When it comes to web tools you use, what kind of UI do you prefer?
A clean and minimal interface for daily use, with detailed insights shown only when necessary. Fast loading and strong visual hierarchy are also important.

### Most Surprising Thing
Very few teams actively compare AI usage against pricing, even though many suspect they are overspending.

### What It Changed About My Design
This interview reinforced the importance of transparency, easy-to-verify recommendations, and visually shareable reports that teams can confidently use internally.
