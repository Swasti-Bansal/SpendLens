# Go To Market

## Exact Target User

Engineering managers and CTOs at seed to Series A startups with 3-15 engineers. They approved the Cursor or Claude subscription 6 months ago and haven't reviewed it since. They feel vaguely guilty about the bill but don't have time to audit it manually.

Not: solo developers (too small, spend too low). Not: enterprise (procurement handles it).

## What They Search or Scroll Before Wanting This

- "cursor vs github copilot 2026"
- "is claude team worth it"
- "ai tools for engineering teams"
- Scrolling Hacker News on a Monday morning
- Reading a tweet thread about someone's dev stack

## Where They Hang Out Online

- Hacker News — Show HN posts consistently reach this audience
- r/ExperiencedDevs and r/startups on Reddit
- Indie Hackers — active founder community
- X/Twitter — following accounts like @levelsio, @marc_louvion, @patio11
- Slack groups: Indie Hackers, Online Geniuses, various YC alumni channels

## First 100 Users in 30 Days — $0 Budget

1. **Show HN post** — "Show HN: I built a free tool that audits your AI tool spend" — target Monday 9am US EST for maximum visibility. One good Show HN can drive 500-2000 visitors.

2. **Twitter/X thread** — "We audited our AI tools and found $340/mo in waste. Here's what we found and how to check yours:" — link to tool at the end. Authentic, specific, not promotional.

3. **Reddit posts** — r/ExperiencedDevs, r/startups — frame as "built this after getting frustrated by our AI bill" — not a pitch, a solution to a shared problem.

4. **Direct DMs to founders** — find 50 founders on X who have tweeted about Cursor or ChatGPT costs. DM with: "Hey, built a free tool that might save you money on AI tools — took me 45 seconds to find $180/mo in waste on my own stack."

5. **Indie Hackers post** — "What I learned building an AI spend audit tool in 7 days" — builders love process posts, and the tool is the payoff.

## Unfair Distribution Channel

Credex has existing relationships with companies selling unused AI credits. Every seller on Credex is a potential user of SpendLens — they're already thinking about AI spend. Seeding SpendLens within the Credex seller network is a channel no independent builder has.
